Implementing stacks Although our register machines use stacks, we need do nothing special here, since stacks can be modeled in terms of lists. e stack can be a list of the saved values, pointed to by a special register the-stack. us, (save ⟨reg⟩) can be implemented as (assign the-stack (op cons)(reg ⟨reg⟩)(reg the-stack)) Similarly, (restore ⟨reg⟩) can be implemented as (assign ⟨reg⟩ (op car)(reg the-stack)) (assign the-stack (op cdr)(reg the-stack)) and (perform (op initialize-stack)) can be implemented as (assign the-stack (const ())) ese operations can be further expanded in terms of the vector opera- tions given above. In conventional computer architectures, however, it is usually advantageous to allocate the stack as a separate vector. en pushing and popping the stack can be accomplished by incrementing or decrementing an index into that vector.
