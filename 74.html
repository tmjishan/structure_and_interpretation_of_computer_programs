As a consequence, these languages can describe iterative processes only by resorting to special-purpose “loop- ing constructs” such as do, repeat, until, for, and while. e imple- mentation of Scheme we shall consider in Chapter 5 does not share this defect. It will execute an iterative process in constant space, even if the iterative process is described by a recursive procedure. An implemen- tation with this property is called tail-recursive.
