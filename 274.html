Notice that the internal procedures here are the same procedures from Section 2.4.1 that Ben wrote when he was working in isolation. No changes are necessary in order to interface them to the rest of the sys- tem. Moreover, since these procedure deﬁnitions are internal to the in- stallation procedure, Ben needn’t worry about name conﬂicts with other procedures outside the rectangular package. To interface these to the rest of the system, Ben installs his real-part procedure under the op- eration name real-part and the type (rectangular), and similarly for the other selectors. 45 e interface also deﬁnes the constructors to be used by the external system. 46 ese are identical to Ben’s internally deﬁned constructors, except that they aach the tag.
