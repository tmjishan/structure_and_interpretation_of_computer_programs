From this point of view, the complex number z = x + iy (where i 2 = -1) can be thought of as the point in the plane whose real coordinate is x and whose imaginary coordinate is y. Addition of complex numbers reduces in this representation to addition of coordinates: Real-part(z 1 + z 2 ) = Real-part(z 1 ) + Real-part(z 2 ) , Imaginary-part(z 1 + z 2 ) = Imaginary-part(z 1 ) + Imaginary-part(z 2 ).
