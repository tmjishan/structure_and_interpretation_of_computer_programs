But streams give us an opportunity to do some interesting tricks. For example, we can transform a stream with a sequence accelerator that converts a sequence of approximations to a new sequence that converges to the same value as the original, only faster. One such accelerator, due to the eighteenth-century Swiss math- ematician Leonhard Euler, works well with sequences that are partial sums of alternating series (series of terms with alternating signs).
