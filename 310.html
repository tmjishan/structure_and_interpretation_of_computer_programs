A more reasonable representation of the term list of a sparse polynomial is as a list of the nonzero terms, where each term is a list containing the order of the term and the coeﬃcient for that order. In such a scheme, polynomial B is eﬃciently represented as ((100 1) (2 2) (0 1)). As most polynomial manipulations are performed on sparse polynomials, we will use this method. We will as- sume that term lists are represented as lists of terms, arranged from highest-order to lowest-order term.
