A very simple grammar might stipulate that a sentence always consists of two pieces— a noun phrase followed by a verb—and that a noun phrase consists of an article followed by a noun. With this grammar, the sentence “e cat eats” is parsed as follows: (sentence (noun-phrase (article the)(noun cat)) (verb eats)) We can generate such a parse with a simple program that has separate procedures for each of the grammatical rules.
