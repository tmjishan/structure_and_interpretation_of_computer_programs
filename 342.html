Sameness and change e issue surfacing here is more profound than the mere breakdown of a particular model of computation. As soon as we introduce change into our computational models, many notions that were previously straight- forward become problematical. Consider the concept of two things be- ing “the same.” Suppose we call make-decrementer twice with the same argument to create two procedures: (define D1 (make-decrementer 25)) (define D2 (make-decrementer 25)) Are D1 and D2 the same? An acceptable answer is yes, because D1 and D2 have the same computational behavior—each is a procedure that sub- tracts its input from 25.
