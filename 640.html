Logic as programs We can regard a rule as a kind of logical implication: If an assignment of values to paern variables satisﬁes the body, then it satisﬁes the con- clusion. Consequently, we can regard the query language as having the ability to perform logical deductions based upon the rules. As an exam- ple, consider the append operation described at the beginning of Section 4.4. As we said, append can be characterized by the following two rules: • For any list y, the empty list and y append to form y. • For any u, v, y, and z, (cons u v) and y append to form (cons u z) if v and y append to form z.
