On the other hand, intermixing delayed evaluation with printing—and, even worse, with assignment—is extremely confusing, and instruc- tors of courses on computer languages have traditionally tormented their students with examination questions such as the ones in this section. Needless to say, writing pro- grams that depend on such subtleties is odious programming style. Part of the power of stream processing is that it lets us ignore the order in which events actually happen in our programs. Unfortunately, this is precisely what we cannot aﬀord to do in the presence of assignment, which forces us to be concerned with time and change. 440
