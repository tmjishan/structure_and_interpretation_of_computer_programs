To estimate the area of the region described by such a predicate, begin by choosing a rectangle that contains the region. For example, a rectangle with diagonally opposite corners at (2, 4) and (8, 10) con- tains the circle above. e desired integral is the area of that portion of the rectangle that lies in the region. We can estimate the integral by picking, at random, points (x , y) that lie in the rectangle, and testing P (x , y) for each point to determine whether the point lies in the region. If we try this with many points, then the fraction of points that fall in the region should give an estimate of the proportion of the rectangle that lies in the region. Hence, multiplying this fraction by the area of the entire rectangle should produce an estimate of the integral.
