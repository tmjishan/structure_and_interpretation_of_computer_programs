How rapidly do these sequences converge? Infinite streams of pairs In Section 2.2.3, we saw how the sequence paradigm handles traditional nested loops as processes deﬁned on sequences of pairs. If we generalize this technique to inﬁnite streams, then we can write programs that are not easily represented as loops, because the “looping” must range over an inﬁnite set. For example, suppose we want to generalize the prime-sum-pairs procedure of Section 2.2.3 to produce the stream of pairs of all integers ( i , j ) with i ≤ j such that i + j is prime.
